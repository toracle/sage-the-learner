= Part 4: Integration and Reflection

== The Completion of Wisdom

[quote]
____
"Where learning ends, wisdom begins."
____

'''

Three years had passed.

Sage no longer lost his way. When he entered an unfamiliar forest, he found his landmarks; before the unknown, he found his connecting threads; even in the company of others, he did not lose himself. What he had learned from the Sage had by now seeped into his very body.

One evening, Sage asked the Sage carefully:

"Have I now learned everything there is to learn?"

The Sage was silent for a long time. Then he spoke.

"No one has ever finished learning. But there are those who have come to know how to put what they have learned to use in the world."

Sage sat for a long time weighing those words.

'''

Part 4 is not the story of Sage completing his learning. It is the story of Sage bringing learning into his life.

Does what was learned inside the forest also hold true outside of it? Does curiosity fade as knowledge accumulates, or does it deepen? And what is a true sage — someone who knows much, or someone who knows how to keep learning?

These final three episodes are the time in which Sage asks himself his deepest questions. At the end of those questions, Sage becomes ready to return to the Valley of Stagnation.
