= Prologue: Leaving the Valley of Stagnation

[quote]
____
"Accumulating knowledge does not build wisdom. Wisdom grows from the way one learns."
____

'''

== The Valley of Stagnation

Once upon a time, there was a village called the Valley of Stagnation, ringed by tall mountains and deep rivers.

As its name suggested, the thinking of its people never moved forward. Sons learned the same way their fathers had, and students memorized word for word whatever their teachers recited. There were many books. The library was full of thick parchment, and scholars measured one another by how many passages they could recite from memory. Knowledge accumulated, yet the village never improved.

In that village lived a young scholar named Sage.

He was more diligent than anyone. He rose at dawn to open his books, and wrote notes by candlelight past midnight. Whatever needed memorizing, he memorized; when there were examinations, he gave his utmost. Strangely, however, the knowledge he had so carefully built seemed to lose its power whenever he faced a real problem.

One summer, a drought struck the village. The elders summoned Sage.

"We hear you have studied hydrology? Can you tell us how to redirect the river's flow?"

Sage worked hard to recall what he had read: formulas for water levels, methods for calculating flow rates, classifications of river terrain… But when he actually stood at the riverbank, the knowledge in his head would not connect with the river before his eyes. He had formulas, but no idea how to apply them. He could not determine which classification the valley fell into, nor which variable to measure first. Nothing linked together.

In the end, the elders sent for an experienced canal builder who solved the problem himself.

That night, Sage sat for a long time staring at the candle flame. Something deep in his chest whispered to him.

_'I do not know how to learn.'_

'''

== Rumor of a Sage

Near the market entrance stood an old apothecary shop. Its owner, an elderly woman, had lived in the village longer than anyone and had traveled farther than anyone else.

When Sage poured out his troubles to her, she thought for a moment and then spoke.

"Have you ever been to the Mountain of Contemplation?"

"The Mountain of Contemplation?"

"Deep in that mountain, they say, lives a wise one. What he teaches cannot be found in books. He teaches the way of learning itself — things you can only know by going there yourself."

The old woman's eyes were serious.

"It won't be easy. The mountain is far and the path is rough. And the wise one, they say, accepts only those who are ready to learn."

Sage could not sleep that night. The woman's words kept circling through his mind. _'The way of learning itself.'_ He did not yet understand what that meant. But he felt it was pointing at something important.

The next morning, Sage packed his worn satchel with a notebook, a few pens, and food for several days. He did not pack books. He had already had enough of those.

He stepped out of the Valley of Stagnation and set his feet toward the Mountain of Contemplation.

'''

== The First Meeting

He walked for three days.

The mountain path was far steeper than he had expected; there were no maps and few people. Sage was leaning exhausted against a rock when a voice came unexpectedly from beside him.

"Where are you from?"

An old man with a white beard was sitting on a tree stump. Sage had not noticed him there at all.

"I've come from the Valley of Stagnation. I'm looking for the Sage."

"The Sage." The old man repeated it slowly. "And what do you hope to find?"

Sage hesitated. To memorize more? To perform better on examinations? No. He already knew how to do those things, and they had done him no good.

"…I want to learn how to learn."

Wrinkles formed at the corners of the old man's eyes. It was a smile.

"That is the hardest, and the most precious, question."

The old man rose and began walking up the mountain.

"Follow me."

In that instant Sage understood. This old man was the Sage himself. He hurried to sling on his pack and followed.

That was how it all began.

'''

_This book contains twenty stories that Sage learned from the Sage. Each story is a small event that took place in the forest, but within it lies a principle about how to learn, connect, share, and ultimately integrate knowledge into wisdom. As you follow Sage's journey, you too will begin to draw your own map of learning._
